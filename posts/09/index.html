A Practical Introduction to Blockchain Technology using Python

A blockchain is a fully-distributed, peer-to-peer software network which makes use of cryptography to securely host applications, store data, and easily transfer digital instruments of value that represent real-world money.

The concept of blockchains was introduced with the invention of Bitcoin in 2008 by Satoshi Nakamoto.

According to the whitepaper, Bitcoin is a peer-to-peer electronic cash system using a decentralised ledger or a blockchain.

Transactions are verified by network nodes through cryptography and recorded in a public distributed ledger called a blockchain.

Bitcoin and most blockchain networks rely on some fundamentals concepts of cryptography like elliptic curve cryptography
